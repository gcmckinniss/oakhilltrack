# Oak Hill Sprints — Claude Config

## Design Context

### Users
High school sprinters (ages 14–18) coached in a competitive track program. They access this site to track personal progress, stay informed on team schedules and results, and feel connected to the program. They are mobile-first, visually driven, and motivated by recognition and achievement. The coach also uses it to communicate and showcase the program.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Elite. Precise. Relentless.

The site represents a serious sprint program that takes its athletes seriously. It should feel like a locker room before a championship race — focused, charged, proud. Not a school portal. Not a generic sports page. This is a program with identity.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Reference:** Nike athlete campaigns — dark, powerful, athlete-centered. Bold type, strong contrast, minimal color.
- **Anti-references:** Generic school websites, pastel/light sport apps, anything that looks like a template or clip art.
- **Theme:** Dark only. Near-black backgrounds (#050505 → #222 depth scale), warm off-white text (#F0EDE8), monochromatic palette.
- **Accent:** Black, white, and minimal grey only. No bright colors. Contrast and typography carry the visual weight.
- **Existing tokens:** `--black: #050505`, `--dark: #0a0a0a`, `--dark2: #111`, `--dark3: #181818`, `--dark4: #222`, `--white: #F0EDE8`, `--dim: #888`, `--muted: #555`, subtle white borders at low opacity.

### Design Principles

1. **Speed is the aesthetic.** Every layout decision should imply forward motion and urgency. Tight spacing, sharp edges, bold type over imagery.

2. **Numbers are the hero.** Personal bests, split times, rankings — these are the emotional core of the product. Display data with reverence: large, clear, easy to read at a glance.

3. **Monochrome earns its power.** No color as decoration. Black and white contrast is the visual language. When something needs emphasis, use scale, weight, and spacing — not color.

4. **Athletes should see themselves.** Every design choice should make the user feel recognized and part of something elite. Avoid generic patterns; lean into specificity and identity.

5. **Elite, not institutional.** This is not a school admin tool. The tone is athlete-first — confident, direct, no fluff. Copy should be short, punchy, and motivating.
